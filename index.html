Most "fresh" bakeries are really just reheating trucked-in dough. Ours is different — mixed, proofed and baked from scratch every morning, the French way, with a Vietnamese soul stirred through every bánh mì, tart and croissant. It's why the Gold Coast just voted us its favourite bakery.

The problem with "fresh"

Supermarket shelves are full of bread. Real bakeries are getting harder to find.

Par-baked loaves, artificial flavouring, a croissant that's more air than butter — it's what passes for a bakery in most shopping strips now. We opened Boulangerie Vietnam because Burleigh Waters deserved better: proper lamination, real fillings, and flavours you won't find anywhere else on the Coast.

Meet the bakers

A Greek boy from Adelaide, a Vietnamese kitchen, and one very stubborn oven.

Jeremy grew up around food but not this food — that part came from his wife, whose family recipes for bánh mì and rice paper rolls now sit on the same counter as his croissants and baguettes. Two very different food cultures, one shopfront in TreeTops, and a shared belief that "good enough" isn't.

"We're not trying to be the fanciest bakery on the Coast. We just want every single thing on that shelf to be something we're proud to hand you." — Jeremy & [wife's name], owners
